# Summary

The model uses a multi-task objective that combines a **Causal Variational Autoencoder (CEVAE)** framework with an **Adversarial Discriminator** via Gradient Reversal. This setup balances data reconstruction, outcome prediction, statistical independence (fairness), and counterfactual cycle-invariance.

The primary VAE network updates its weights by minimising a combined loss, utilising the **GradNorm** algorithm to dynamically balance the reconstruction, adversarial and counterfactual invariance tasks:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{VAE\_Total}} = w_{GradNorm\_0} \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{recon}} \\+ w_{GradNorm\_1} \cdot w_{\text{TC}} \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{TC}} \\ + w_{GradNorm\_2} \cdot w_{\text{CF\_invar}} \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{CF\_invar}} \\ +  w_{\text{kl}} \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{kl}} + w_{\text{distill}} \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{distill}}$$

# Loss Components & Hyperparameters

## 1. Rebalanced reconstruction loss ($\mathcal{L}_{\text{recon}}$)

Measures the model's ability to reconstruct the S-biased features ($X_{\text{desc}}$) and predict the binary target outcome ($Y$) from the abducted latent space $U_{desc}$.

Combines feature-specific reconstruction errors (Cross-Entropy for categorical, BCE for binary, MSE for continuous) with a minimax-style weighted outcome prediction loss across sensitive subgroups ($S$).

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{recon}} = \alpha_{\text{desc}} \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{desc\_recon}} + \alpha_{\text{pred}} \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{weighted\_y}}$$

* `args.desc_a` ($\alpha_{\text{desc}}$): Weighting coefficient for feature reconstruction.
* `args.pred_a` ($\alpha_{\text{pred}}$): Weighting coefficient for target prediction.



### Adaptive minimax outcome reconstruction ($\mathcal{L}_{weighted\_y}$)

The weighted outcome reconstruction (prediction) loss $\mathcal{L}_{\text{weighted\_y}}$ implements a minimax optimisation over the sensitive demographic subgroups. This forces the network to minimise the loss for the worst-performing group, preventing the model from maximising overall accuracy by sacrificing predictive performance on minority groups.

- **Stratified subgroup losses:** For each sensitive ethnic group $g \in \{0, 1, \dots, G-1\}$, the unweighted Binary Cross-Entropy (BCE) loss $\mathcal{L}_{\text{BCE}}^{(g)}$ is computed exclusively over the samples belonging to that stratum.

- **Dynamic group weights via Softmax:** To track and heavily penalize the group exhibiting the worst predictive performance, a group weight vector $W = [w_0, w_1, \dots, w_{G-1}]^T$ is calculated at the end of each epoch, to account for all batches. This vector is computed by passing the tracked group losses through a parameterised Softmax function:

$$w_g = \frac{\exp(\eta \cdot \bar{\mathcal{L}}_{\text{EMA}}^{(g)})}{\sum_{j=0}^{G-1} \exp(\eta \cdot \bar{\mathcal{L}}_{\text{EMA}}^{(j)})}$$

$\eta$ is the scaling hyperparameter controlled by `args.group_eta`. Higher values of $\eta$ shift the optimisation into a strict minimax objective by assigning near-total weight to the group with the highest error.

$\bar{\mathcal{L}}_{\text{EMA}}^{(g)}$ is the historical Exponential Moving Average (EMA) of group $g$'s loss, updated at each batch step to maintain stable gradient directions:

$$\bar{\mathcal{L}}_{\text{EMA\_new}}^{(g)} = (1 - \gamma) \cdot \bar{\mathcal{L}}_{\text{EMA\_old}}^{(g)} + \gamma \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{BCE}}^{(g)}$$

with $\gamma = 0.1$

- **Weighted outcome loss:** The final outcome reconstruction loss passed to the main VAE objective is the dot product of the detached dynamic weights and the current weight's stratified group losses:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{weighted\_y}} = \sum_{g=0}^{G-1} \text{detach}(w_g) \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{BCE}}^{(g)}$$

The weights $w_g$ are explicitly detached from the autograd graph (with `torch.no_grad():`) during this step. This ensures that the encoder and decoder are optimised to reduce the actual prediction errors of the targeted groups, rather than artificially lowering the overall loss by mathematically manipulating the weight coefficients.

### Group-entropy weighted feature reconstruction ($\mathcal{L}_{desc\_recon}$)

The descriptive feature reconstruction loss $\mathcal{L}_{\text{desc\_recon}}$ addresses inequalities in information density across demographic cohorts. Optimising for raw reconstruction error can cause the VAE to favor groups with higher-entropy feature profiles which bear more importance in the gradient backpropagation and mobilise more capacity in the latent than the simpler lower-entropy groups.

To counteract this, your model scales each subgroup's feature reconstruction loss by its inverse group entropy, forcing the network to allocate sufficient capacity to each group.

- **Baseline entropy estimation:** Before training begins, the Shannon entropy of the feature space ($X_{\text{desc}}$) is computed independently for each sensitive group $g \in \{0, 1, \dots, G-1\}$: $H(X_{\text{desc}} \mid S = g)$. This baseline is robustly estimated using a bootstrapping procedure and uses Miller-Madow with K+ to mitigate sample bias.

- **Weight derivation:** The weights assigned to each group's reconstruction loss are inversely proportional to their empirical entropy. This ensures that groups are penalised on a similar scale regardless of their feature complexity.

$$\tilde{w}_g = \frac{1}{H(X_{\text{desc}} \mid S = g)}$$

- **Normalised weights:** These values are normalised across all sensitive groups to sum to 1, yielding a static weight vector $D = [d_0, d_1, \dots, d_{G-1}]^T$

$$d_g = \frac{\tilde{w}_g}{\sum_{j=0}^{G-1} \tilde{w}_j}$$

- **Stratifed reconstruction;** For each sensitive ethnic group $g \in \{0, 1, \dots, G-1\}$, the total feature reconstruction loss $\mathcal{L}_{\text{desc\_recon}}^{(g)}$ is computed exclusively over the samples belonging to that stratum.

- **Weighted reconstruction loss:** computed as the dot product between the static normalised inverse-entropy weights ($d_g$) and the current batch's stratified feature reconstruction losses:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{desc\_recon}} = \sum_{g=0}^{G-1} d_g \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{desc\_recon}}^{(g)}$$

Unlike the dynamic minimax outcome weights, `desc_entropy_weights` are entirely static.


## 2. Adversarial Total Correlation Loss ($\mathcal{L}_{\text{tc}}$)

Enforces statistical independence between the latent representation ($U_{desc}$) and the sensitive attribute ($S$)

Passes $U_{desc}$ through a Gradient Reversal Layer (GRL) into a Spectral-Normalised Discriminator. The encoder is penalised if the discriminator can successfully predict ethnicity from $U_{desc}$.

**Involved Hyperparameters:**
* `args.tc_warm_up`: Epoch threshold for the linear annealing of the gradient reversal magnitude.
* `grad_rev_alpha` ($\alpha$): The current epoch's multiplier for reversed gradients (annealed up to `4.0`).


## 3. Latent Counterfactual Invariance Loss ($\mathcal{L}_{\text{cf\_invar}}$)

Guarantees cycle-invariance by ensuring that an individual's latent profile remains unchanged under a counterfactual flip of their sensitive attribute.

Computes the bidirectional KL divergence between the true abducted latent distribution $q(U_d \mid X_{\text{desc}}, S, Y)$ and the distribution obtained by encoding the generated counterfactual world $q(U_d \mid X_{\text{desc\_cf}}, 1-S, Y_{\text{cf\_prob}})$.


## 4. KL Divergence Prior Regularisation ($\mathcal{L}_{\text{kl}}$)

Forces the abducted latent space from the training encoder to conform to a standard normal prior distribution.

Standard VAE latent regularisation via $\mathcal{D}_{\text{KL}}(q(U_d \mid X_{\text{desc}}, S, Y) \parallel \mathcal{N}(0, I))$.

**Involved Hyperparameters:**
* `args.kl_warm_up`: Epoch threshold used to linearly anneal $w_{\text{kl}}$ from `0.0` to `1.0`.


## 5. Distributional distillation loss ($\mathcal{L}_{\text{distill}}$)

Bridges the gap between training-time "abduction" (which has access to ground-truth labels $Y$) and deployment-time "inference" (which must operate without $Y$).

Forces the deployment inference encoder $q(U_d \mid X_{\text{desc}}, S)$ to match the distribution of the optimized training encoder. Formulated as a bilateral KL divergence between adbuction and inference latents.

**Involved Hyperparameters:**
* `args.distill_warm_up`: Epoch threshold used to linearly anneal $w_{\text{distill}}$ from `0.0` to `1.0`.

# Optimization & Gradient Balancing

## GradNorm 

Rather than using static joint weights, the main VAE architecture balances tasks 1, 2, and 3 using dynamic task weights ($w_0, w_1, w_2$).

Calculates the $L_2$ norm of the gradients for each individual task with respect to a selected shared layer (`model.encoder_desc[-2].weight`, i.e. the last Linear layer of the encoder). It optimises the task weights via an auxiliary Adam weight update algorithm (`grad_optimiser`) to balance training rates across tasks.

**Involved Hyperparameters:**
* `args.gradnorm_gamma` ($\gamma$): Asymmetry parameter that dictates the strength of the pull towards equal training rates across tasks.
* `args.vae_lr`: Learning rate for both the main VAE network and the weight-balancing optimizer.


## Independent Discriminator Objective

The discriminator is trained entirely outside of the GradNorm pipeline on a separate objective (`discrim_optimiser`) using label-smoothed weighted BCE with logits, to take sensitive group imbalance in the dataset into account:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{disc}} = -\frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \left[ p_{\text{weight}} \cdot s_i \cdot \log \sigma(\mathcal{D}(u_{d, i})) + (1 - s_i) \cdot \log (1 - \sigma(\mathcal{D}(u_{d, i}))) \right]$$

Where:
- $p_{weight} = N_{majority} / N_{minority}$ 
- $s_i$ is the true sensitive attribute (ethnicity label $0$ or $1$).
- $\mathcal{D}(u_{d, i})$ is the raw scalar logit outputted by the discriminator network for the latent code $u_{d, i}$.
- $\sigma(\cdot)$ is the standard sigmoid activation function converting the logit to an estimated probability

**Involved Hyperparameters:**
* `args.disc_lr`: Learning rate for the discriminator parameters.
* `args.disc_step`: Frequency parameter; the discriminator updates only once every $N$ training batches.
